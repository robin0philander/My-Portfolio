ABOUT

Hi, my name is Robin Philander


I'm an IT - Software Development graduate, and a qualified fullstack web developer. I have gained knowledge about how to code both the front-end & back-end of websites throughout the years, using different IDE's, coding languages and libraries/frameworks.

I also know a few things about computer hardware and it's peripherals. But that's not the only area i excel in, i'm also on a journey to become a self-taught Graphic Designer and an aspiring Video Game Developer.


Country of Origin: South Africa

Home Town: Uitenhage

High School attended: Uitenhage Secondary School

College attended: Eastcape Midlands College

University attended: Nelson Mandela Metropolitan University

Bio: I’m a creative person by heart, and i’ve always had a keen eye for design or anything that sparks my creative mind. I enjoy finding solutions to problems. Some days i can go for hours non-stop trying to figure out why some things work and why others don’t. When i do finally get a solution or at least discover why something doesn’t work, i feel a sense of pride and accomplishment from it. Which always makes the amount of time i spent on it, just so much more worth it. It’s both a healthy and unhealthy obsession of mine. One can even call it an addiction. The same goes with when i finish an art or design project i’m working on. Just seeing the end result gets me in a state of bliss.

After High School i went to college at EMC (Eastcape Midlands College) to study Information Technology and Computer Science, and after the 3 year course, i graduated and got my NQF Level 2, 3 & 4 certificates. I then went to NMMU (Nelson Mandela Metropolitan University) to study for my Diploma in IT – Software Development, in which i finished in 3 years and graduated.

After exiting my first job as a Customer Support Representative(Technical Call Center Agent) at WNS Global Services, while at home i decided to pursue further studies online. Where i kept my coding skills up to date, through online courses and by creating small games & websites…i then also decided to learn graphic design since i've always admired it and have been curious to try it. After a year or so, of not being able to score the dream job that i wanted. I got my graphic design skills to a decent enough level, to where i was able to make an income from it. I worked on a few projects for clients (friends, family and local businesses) that wanted me to design for them. I also made an income in various other ways, by helping clients in the following areas: Troubleshooting and fixing minor computer problems, Building and piecing together computers and computer components, Replacing old or faulty hardware, Rebooting/Wiping computer harddrives and installing new operating systems, Setting up people's LTE Wi-Fi Routers and changing their security settings, Setting up a clients smart phone as well updating & downloading the necesarry software/apps they needed or requested.

and that leads us up to now…
